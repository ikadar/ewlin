Selection Style Comparison REQ-01

Four alternatives for indicating the selected job's tiles. Each shown on a default tile, then on a conflict tile to verify it doesn't mask state color.

A) Luminance boost

The tile brightens — feels "active." Cheap, clean, no extra elements. Risk: may wash out state color on light states.

B) White left border

The left border turns bright white, widened to 5px. Leverages existing visual language (border = tile identity). Minimal, precise.

C) Soft neutral glow

Subtle white glow, much softer than the old outline. Feels warm and intentional, doesn't look like a focus ring.

D) Elevated lift

Tile lifts slightly (scale + shadow). Physical metaphor — "picked up." Risk: may cause layout jitter in tight grids.

E) Pulsating background

A gentle white pulse breathes through the tile. Draws the eye through motion rather than static contrast. Subtle enough not to distract, but unmissable on a still grid.

F) Pulse + Luminance

Combines both: the tile is permanently brighter (establishes selection at a glance) and pulses gently (catches peripheral vision). Double signal — static + motion.

Combined States

States can overlap — a tile can be both selected and conflicting, or completed and selected. The system handles combinations cleanly.
