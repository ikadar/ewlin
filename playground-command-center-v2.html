Command Center v2

Reference + Dropdown Zones stay static — typing opens a filtered dropdown on top

Layout Variants

V1 — 2-Column Zones
6 category cards in 2 columns. Dropdown overlays when typing. Clean and balanced.
V2 — 3-Column Dense
Maximum shortcut density. 3 narrow columns. Best for large sets.
V3 — Masonry Cards
Variable-height zones with subtle borders. Premium feel, natural sizing.
V4 — 2-Col + Context
Context header (selected job) above zones. Dropdown still overlays on type.

Expert Notes

Sophia — UX

This is the right model. The zones are a passive reference — always there, always the same. The dropdown is the active interaction. Two layers, two purposes.

Marcus — Keyboard

Love it. Alt+K opens the center, I glance at the zones to remember a shortcut, or I type to execute. Zero friction. The zones teach me, the dropdown serves me.

Lea — Design

Key detail: dim the zones slightly when the dropdown opens. Focus the eye on the dropdown without hiding the reference. The blue top-border on the dropdown is the visual separator.

Jean-Pierre — Print

The operator opens the center, sees "ah yes, Alt+Q for placement", closes it and uses the shortcut directly. Next time, muscle memory. The zones are training wheels that never go away.

Behavior

Empty field: Zones visible, no dropdown.
Typing: Dropdown opens with filtered results. Zones stay unchanged behind (dimmed).
Esc / clear: Dropdown closes, zones return to full opacity.
Execute: Click or Enter runs the command and closes.
